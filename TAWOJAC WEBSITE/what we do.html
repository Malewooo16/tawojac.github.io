What we do

Women and Children Testing and Treatment

On the 16/08/2022 TAWOJAC in collaboration with the District Commissioner's Office in Kibaha City; Hon. Sara Msafiri, and partnership with the Tanzania People's Defence Forces (JWTZ) led by Lt Colonel Mwalukunga (MD) Lugalo Military Hospital and the Office of the Chief Medical Officer in Pwani Region conducted a free women and children and health screening and treatment exercise at Kibaha district.

TAWOJAC successfully coordinated a free health screening and treatment excercise for women and children at Maili Moja stand More than 300 women were screened for various communicable diseases,the guest of honor was the Deputy Minister of Information and Communication and Technology Hon. Kundo A. Mathew. The exercise focused on testing women for breast cancer, malaria, skin diseases. Vaccines for children were also administered along with the COVID 19 vaccine, with at least 70 children screened for malaria, skin diseases and worm infestations. Children were treated while others were vaccinated.

AIMS FOR THIS ACTIVITY
  • To conduct frequent health testing activities in many regions of Tanzania for longer periods up to 5 days
  • Raising awareness of major health issues affecting women such as cervical and breast cancer
  • Immunizing children against diseases such as Polio and Measles
  • Advocating issues of hygiene, sanitation and balanced diets to reduce the number frequently occuring diseases due to the lack of important health practices

Women Empowerment Initiative

Women Empowerment Initiative (WEI), is an approach devised by TAWOJAC aimed at building the much needed entrepreneurial and investment capacity of Tanzanian women thereby increasing their financial worth. Womens increased financial capacity will positively impact on family quality of life.

As it is witnessed that in Tanzania although women make a large population of working individuals most of them are inactive in the economy, this issue leads to the increase in family deprivations and increasing levels of poverty

WEI focuses on building the entrepreneurial capacity of women by training them to be skillfull in producing goods and services, proper business modeling to convert their dreams to reality and to understand important financial services such as taking out loans.

TAWOJAC is open to empower all women in Dar-es-Salaam region who wish to become active in the economy by conducting workshops, seminars and linking them towards opportunites to nature their businesses.TAWOJAC alo encourages a strong partnership of coprates and institutions, to collaboratively aid in the empowerment of Tanzanian women

AIMS FOR THIS ACTIVITY
  • Having a large number of Tanzanian women who are active in the ecomony
  • Equiping women with important skills suck as digital networking and branding to futher expand their businesses
  • Linking the women with other successful women for mentorship and expansion.
  • Enabling women to particpate in workshops and showrooms as producers to inspire other women who wish to become econmoically active
