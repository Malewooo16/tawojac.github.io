WHO WE ARE

Tanzania Women Journalists Advocate for Children Rights (TAWOJAC), is a Non-governmental organization registered under Act No. 24 of 2002 under the Ministry of Community Development, Gender, Women and Special Groups. Our motto stating " ensuring that Tanzania has women and children, who are free, secure, healthy and well educated " TAWOJAC deals with protecting the rights of women and children and combating all form of discrimination towards them.

TAWOJAC employes the use of journalists and a variety of people with commendable experience in the media, and media engagement.

This is an added strength to the communication and advocacy focus in all activities where broad media coverage on mainstream and social media will be an added communication, advocacy and sensitisation dimension covering most regions of Tanzania through the regional press clubs.

Executive statement

For many years, my fellow reporters and I have covered a variety of community problems that affect women and children, but only a few our stories and exposes have had a beneficial effect because the problems are persistently covered but not resolved. This was the turning point to create an NGO with experienced journalists, to have a vast amount of information about issues affecting women and children in the community, and to create solutions.

Executive Director Neema Nchimbi
